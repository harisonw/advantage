## The business problem

A UK motor finance company processes roughly 1,600 vehicle finance deals per month. Each deal involves multiple documents arriving from different parties: the broker submits an application, the finance company generates an HP agreement, the dealer sends a purchase invoice, supplier declaration, giro slip, and funds form, and the customer signs a payment mandate. There are also external system checks (FCA register lookups, vehicle finance checks, bank verification).

Today, a team of payout administrators manually checks every deal by comparing data across all of these documents. This manual process takes 10-15 minutes per deal. Problems are found roughly 5% of the time. The rest of the time, everything matches and the human check adds no value. The company processes around 80 deals per day.

We are building a system to replace this manual process. The core concept is a **field reconciliation engine**: every deal has a set of fields (customer name, vehicle details, financial figures, dealer information, etc.), and each document contributes values for a subset of those fields. The system cross-references all sources and flags where things don't line up.

The goal is to **minimise the amount of human work needed to get deals through the pipeline**, with a view to automating the process entirely over time. Where everything is clean, the system should handle it end to end. Where there are genuine problems, the right people need to know about them quickly and clearly so they can act.

## Your task

Build a **frontend prototype** that demonstrates how the people in this business would interact with the reconciliation system for a single deal.

You'll need to figure out:

- Who the users are and what they're trying to accomplish
- What the data in the documents means and what matters
- How to present the reconciliation in a way that minimises the work people have to do

### What we're testing

1. **Problem decomposition.** Can you go from a business problem description and raw data to a clear set of jobs to be done?
2. **Scoping.** You have 2 hours. What do you build to demonstrate the core value? What do you leave out? Can you explain those trade-offs?
3. **UX and product sense.** Does the interface actually help users do their jobs? The measure of success is minimising the human effort needed to process deals, with the longer-term goal of full automation.
4. **Effective use of AI tools.** We expect you to use AI coding tools (Cursor, Claude, Copilot, etc.) to build this. How effectively you leverage them is part of the evaluation.
